MOSES MOODY

Scientific Name:

Emotio-apollus

Discovered by / at:

Stephen Hawking in 1974

Family:

Being made up of microorganisms and light particles, which are predominantly stars and photons, the Emotio-apollous is found to be closely related to planets and moons and a distant relative of asteroids!

Habitat:

Having been discovered by Stephen Hawking during his research on black holes in 1974, Moody was a big part of proving Hawking’s theory that black holes aren’t completely black. As the only living organism in the hole, he spends his time floating and bouncing around to pass the time.

Appearance:

Although his size is constantly changing, Moody is always circle-shaped and the color of his face is always dependent on his mood.

Key Characteristics:

  • Unpredictable, can be super moody
  • Can speak every language in the world
  • Smells like the men's locker room
  • Big fan of Spiderman

What do we know so far about the creature?

As a part of proving Hawking’s theory that black holes aren’t completely black, his theory postulates that ​​black holes can indeed emit light, or more specifically, radiation. This radiation, in turn, gradually drains energy in the form of light particles that spring up around black holes' potent gravitational fields. This creature was born on August 6, 1945, following the atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki. Because of how black holes work, it is technically a 1-year-old because 80 Earth years is 1 year in a black hole. Created by the mushroom-shaped cloud-developed light organisms that transported at the speed of light and entered into a black hole, Moses Moody is named after the Warriors rookie NBA player because of the various emotions and moods he shows. Living amid various cosmic particles, Moody wastes away his days by roaming the hole trying to look for a new friend.

How do we interact with this creature?

You can interact with this creature by controlling what each face is feeling and the amount of heads the creature will have.If you move the mouse up and down, the creature will change its mood. If you hold onto the mouse, the creature will get dizzy and rotate around. You can also tickle him by clicking the mouse rapidly and he will do a little shimmy dance.
